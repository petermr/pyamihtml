of cooperation: (1) security (including

and (3) humanitarian cooperation (food/
water security and basic protections). This
approach draws from the Helsinki process
that created the Organization for Security and
Cooperation in Europe almost 50 years ago, but
would be updated to reflect the far-broader range
of risks to regional and global stability today.171
It would align with recent Security Council
discussions on the need for a more strategic
relationship with regional bodies, allowing
Member States and regional organizations to
reinvigorate and broaden their relationships.172
