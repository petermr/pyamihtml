TABLE 2 AGGREGATOR TECHNOLOGIES: PROGNOSIS AND RECOMMENDATIONS FOR GPGS Aggregator Prognosis Recommendations Summation •Free-riding tendency stems from the perfect substitutability of contributions. •Prisoners’ Dilemma (PD) game structure applies. •General tendency is for underprovision.
•Grants and loans are needed to support provision. •Multilateral institutions need support supply. •Repeated interaction may ameliorate PD concerns.
Weighted sum •Less of a tendency for underprovision since one country’s provision is not a perfect substitute for that of another country. •Countries with larger impacts are incentivized to act.
•Institute monitoring to gather information on countries’ supply influence. •Spatial considerations may be essential.
Weakest link •Efficient if countries possess same tastes and GDP. •More equal income distribution promotes provision. •Matching contributions are desired. •There is a need to shore up weakest links, which poses free-riding concerns.
•Capacity building is essential when countries differ. •Global institutions, dominant country, partnerships, and others can assist weakest-links countries.
Weaker link •Efficient if countries are sufficiently similar. •Matching and non-matching behavioral outcomes are relevant. •There is a reduced need to shore up weakest links.
•Some capacity building is required. •Global institutions, dominant country, and partnerships, and others can help to some extent.
Threshold •Threshold creates incentives to contribute. •Reaching the threshold presents a coordination problem but there are still free-riding incentives. •Leadership promotes coordination.
•Multilateral institutions can induce countries to be threshold contributors, thereby offering coordination. •Dominant country or institution can provide leaders.
Best shot •Global income inequality promotes provision. •Multiple best shooters results in a coordination problem. •Poor regions may not possess a best shooter.
•Rich or dominant country fosters provision. •Multilateral organizations and others can pool actions. •Regions must coordinate their provision activity.
Better shot •Multiple countries can contribute. •Contributions do not have to be coordinated or concentrated. •Provisions beneath the largest may be desirable.
•Reduced need for rich country leadership. •The pooling of contributions is less of a necessity. •Less provision supply by outsiders may be required unless rich countries are uninterested.
