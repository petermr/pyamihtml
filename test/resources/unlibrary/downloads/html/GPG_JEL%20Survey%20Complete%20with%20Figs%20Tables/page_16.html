motivate the GPG of relieving world poverty (Anand 2004, Kanbur, Sandler, and Morrison 1999,

With joint products, there are at least three goods in the country’s utility function.
Suppose that these goods are a private numéraire good, a jointly produced pure public good, and
a jointly produced contributor-specific good. The presence of three goods means that the joint
products may be Hicksian substitutes or complements, so that – holding utility constant – an
increase in the price of the private good will increase (decrease) the demand for the country-
specific good that is jointly produced with the country’s public good contribution. In the case of
gross complements, Cornes and Sandler (1984a, 1994) show that increased contributions by
others to the public good may induce a contributor to raise its own contributions in order to get
