Foreign policies require a coherent policy agenda for sustainable poverty
reduction and dealing with global environmental change, both within the
Netherlands and internationally. In this paper we explore the possible
contribution of the global public good perspective to the development of
coherent Dutch policies for international cooperation on poverty reduction
and the global environment. We specify why global public goods are of
interest to the Netherlands for international cooperation, and what the
policy choices are arising from taking a global public goods perspective on
the issues of poverty reduction and the environment.
