The Shared Socioeconomic Pathways (SSPs)

First published in 2017, the Shared Socioeconomic Pathways (SSPs) are alternative projections of socio - economic developments that may influence future GHG emissions.

The initial set of SSP narratives described worlds with different challenges to mitigation and adaptation: SSP1 ( sustainability ), SSP2 ( middle of the road ), SSP3 ( regional rivalry ), SSP4 ( inequality ) and SSP5 ( rapid growth ).
