M ost of the scenarios in the AR6 database are SSP - based . T h e majority of the assessed scenarios are consistent with SSP2. Using the SSPs permit s a more systematic assessment of future GHG emissions and their uncertainties than was possible in AR5. The main emissions drivers across the SSPs include growth in popula tion reaching 8.5 - 9.7 billion by 2050, and an increase in global GDP of 2.7 - 4.1% per year between 2015 and 2050. Final energy demand in the absence of any new climate policies is projected to grow to around 480 to 750 EJ yr - 1 in 2050 (compared to around 39 0 EJ in 2015) (medium confidence) . The highest emissions scenarios in the literature result in global warming of >5 ° C by 2100, based on assumptions of rapid economic growth and pervasive climate policy failures.
