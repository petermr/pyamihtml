To be effective, SEL interventions and skill development should occur in a safe, caring, supportive,
participatory and well-managed environment ‒ that is, an environment that supports students’ development and lets them practise the skills they learn. The learning context encompasses such factors as communication styles, performance expectations, classroom structures and rules, school organizational climate, commitment to the academic success for all students, district policies, and parental and community involvement.
