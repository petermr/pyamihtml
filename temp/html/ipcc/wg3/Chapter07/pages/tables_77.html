3 Avoiding unsustainable intensification
4 It is critical that intensification does not drive expansion of unsustainable practices. Increased productivity with associated economic
5 reward could incentivise and reward agricultural land expansion, or environmentally and socially damaging practices on existing\nand former agricultural land (Ceddia et al. 2013; Phalan 2018). Accordingly, coordinated policies are crucial to ensuring desired\noutcomes (Godfray and Garnett 2014; Reddy et al. 2020; Kassam and Kassam 2020). Barretto et al. (2013) found that in agriculturally\nconsolidated areas, land-use intensification coincided with either a contraction of both cropland and pasture areas, or cropland\nexpansion at the expense of pastures, both resulting in a stable farmed area. In contrast, in agricultural frontier areas, land-use\nintensification coincided with expansion of agricultural lands.
6 In conclusion, SI within agriculture is needed given the rising global population and the need to address multiple environmental\nand social externalities associated with agricultural activities. However, implementation requires strong stakeholder engagement,\nappropriate regulations, rigorous monitoring and verification and comprehensive outreach and knowledge exchange programmes.
